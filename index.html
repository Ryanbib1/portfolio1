Project Fuego

Phase 1 Course Goals


1. Foster the creative habits of mind

This phase definitely pushed me to think more like a creator instead of just a student. Instead of just doing the assignment, I started thinking about the "why" behind my design choices. It's less about just getting it done and more about making something I'm actually proud of.

2. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

The workshop sessions were super helpful. Getting feedback from classmates pointed out things I would've never seen myself. It's one thing to think your site looks good, but it's another to hear how other people actually experience it.

3. Think deeply about craft.

I used to think that the web design was just about making things look cool, but now I learn so much more. It's not only about code is structured, but also the user experience feels. I spent a lot of time to make button to look just right, and after I check my final draft, it was totally worth it. I bring myself as a viewer, it truely interested me to get into it.

4. Engage in sustained research and skills development.

My browser history is now 90% 'how to center a div', html useful code and other questions about html, haha. This phase was a deep dive into self-teaching. I spent a ton of time on sites like W3Schools and other learning website, learning new HTML tags and CSS tricks. It feels good to solve a problem by finding the answer myself.

5. Learn to present your work

Putting everything what we have done in past three week together in this portfolio is the perfect example of this. It is not just about dumping my projects onto a page; it is also about creating a narrative, explaining my process, and showing what I've learned. It is like building a story about my skills.
